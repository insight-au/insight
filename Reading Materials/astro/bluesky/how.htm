LOCATING THE POLE STAR

Imagine locating the pole star only by observing all stars in the sky and keeping track of their movement. Doing so will prove to be very difficult as there are countless stars in the sky. To make this task easy, we make use of the constellation Saptharishi Mandala.

Saptarishi Mandala, as the name indicates, is a group of seven stars named after seven sages. The individual stars are Marichi, Vasishta, Angirasa, Atri, Pulatsya, Pulaha and Kratu. Very near to Vashishta, one can find a faint little star called Arundhathi, his wife. They are arranged as follows. They are best seen in the period  from March to September. It's also called the Great Bear or Big Dipper.

Now concentrate on the two stars Pulaha and Kratu at one end. Draw a line and extend it. You will find The Dhruva tara or the Pole Star on the line.
