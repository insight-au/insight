Examples in Refinement

A precise definition should be both sufficient and complete ,i.e, it shouldn't cover something outside its domain nor should it leave out uncovered something in its domain.

Here are some examples to help you . These examples are exclusively designed to illustrate the actual difficulties in classifying and the methods to overcome them.

1)Nectar:

We can define Nectar as "a sweet liquid" but this leads to ativyapthi i.e., this definition covers all classes of sweet liquids and not nectar alone. (e.g. glucose, honey, ...). So, to complete it, we add its other characteristics like :
naturally produced by and found in flowers.

2)Herbivore:

Herbivore is an animal that which eats grass only. But this gives rise to avyapthi because this doesn't cover all herbivorous animals like human vegetarians.So,we can redefine it as :
"All organisms that feed on plant and plant products."
But, this leads to ativyapthi because besides covering herbivores it also cover omnivores.So,the right way to define is:
"Herbivores are those organisms that feed on plant and plant products only."

3)Hair:

"Dead cells that are found on animal body." By now,you must have recognized that this naturally leads to ativyapti as nails are also dead cells that are found on animal body. So, let's refine :
" Hairs are those dead cells that are found on animal body, are flexible,fine stranded and are coloured."

Well, all things must be in place now.  Here are some more self-explanatory examples.

4) Indians are those who live in India other than the immigrants.

Avyapthi..... : Indians may also live in other parts of world.
Redefinition.: Those who live in India other than the immigrants and those who were born in India and have emigrated from India.

5) BasketBall game:

Game played with a ball between two teams, each trying to put the ball in the
opposing team's target area.
Ativyapthi: Covers football too.
Redefinition: It is a game played  with a large ball between two teams, each trying to the ball into  the opposition's net fixed on a high metal ring.

6) Moon:

Body that revolves around earth.
Ativyapthi: Artificial satellites also revolves earth.
Redefinition: Heavenly body that revolves around earth.

You must now have been convinced that classification is an incremental and iterative process.You have to build up on your definitions step by step such that in each step you add a specific property to make the definition complete.
