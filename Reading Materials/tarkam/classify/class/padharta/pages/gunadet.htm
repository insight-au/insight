Color
Color is referred to as 'rupa' in Sanskrit. Pronounced as ruu-pa. It is perceived by the eye.
Example : When we say a Leaf is green the greenness of the leaf is perceived by the eye and is identified.

This Color is primarily divided into 7:

White Blue

Yellow

Red
Green Brown

Variegated    (  Mixture   of   Colors  )

Of these    
    - Earth has all the 7 colors.
    - Water is pale white
    - Light is bright white

Taste
Taste is referred to as rasa in Sanskrit. Pronounced as Ra-sa.
It is perceived by the tongue.

Taste as perceived by the tongue is divided into 6:

Sweet Acid Saline
Pungent Astringent Bitter

Taste is the characteristic of earth and water of which naturally occurring water is sweet.

Example:
Food, juice, syrup, nectar all have a distinct taste..

Smell
Smell is referred to as gandha in Sanskrit (Pronounced as gan-dha)
It is perceived by the nose.
Some examples are fragrance, scent, coffee etc.

It  is of 2 types : Pleasant and Foul smell.

It occurs in earth alone.
All objects which is some form of earth posses a distinct smell.

Back

 

Touch

Touch as perceived by the skin is of 3 types:

- Cold as in natural water

- Hot as in a steaming cup of coffee

- Luke Warm as in the air

All material substances except space have an element of touch involved in them

Examples:

A hot plate, Ice, Sharp knife are some examples of things one can perceive by touch.

Sound
Sound is referred to as shabdha in Sanskrit (Pronounced as shab-dam)
It is perceived by the ear and is the characteristic feature of space.
It is present in space only

 

It is of two types:

Inarticulate - Sounds produced by nature, sound of air and waves,cries of animals;

Alphabetic - That produced by speaking.

Quantity

It is known as parimaana in Sanskrit.
It is of 4 types:

- Atomic - infinitesimally small in size.

- Large  - Significantly bigger in size.

- Long   - Concerned with larger distance.

- Short   - Distance of an atomic nature

Remoteness and Proximity (paratva and aparatva in Sanskrit)

These are of 2 types :

- Spatial       - With respect to distance.

- Temporal   - With respect to time.

They exist in earth, water, light, air and mind.
Both the terms are relative in nature.

Heaviness

It is referred to as gurutva in Sanskrit.

It is the cause of the first downward movement of objects, in other words, the weight of an object. 

Usage of words like kilograms and pounds are due to this property.

Examples:
It is found in earth, water and all earthly substances.

Back

 

Fluidity

Fluidity is referred to as dravatva in Sanskrit (Pronounced as dra-vath-va).

It is that characteristic which causes fluids to flow. It is common to all matter that have an element of water in it. Though predominantly associated with liquids it is also found in earth and light.

It is of 2 types :

Natural -

Flow of water

Artificial -

Artificial flow of earth found in melted ghee
Artificial flow of light found in molten metals

Stickiness

It is referred to as sneha in Sanskrit.

It resides in water and all forms of water

Some examples are:

Gum , Latex, Sap from wood, Molten plastic, Paste, etc.

Numeric

It is always expressed in a magnitude ranging from 1 to infinity.

It exists in one of these two forms.

- Eternal in atoms

- Non-eternal in all other items

Oneness is eternal and non-eternal.

Anything more that one is non-eternal.

Unlike quantity it is expressed specifically.

Cognition (Knowledge)

It is two fold:
* Remembrance - Knowledge born of mental impressions
* Apprehension - Knowledge other than recollection

Remembrance is recollection of what one already knows.  It is referred to as smart.

Apprehension on the other hand is not mere recollection of what one knows, but correlation of what one observes and understands. It is of 2 kinds:

  • Invalid apprehension - that which arises due to a false understanding, elusive definitions and unreal facts
  • Valid apprehensions - are based on one's analytical knowledge, what one perceives, or hears from a reliable source.
